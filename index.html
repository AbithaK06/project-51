Camel

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  3. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Chameleons

  • The panther chameleon changes its color to yellow or red when it's angry.
  • Chameleons have a lightning-fast tongue.
  • Sick chameleons look pale in color.

Pigeons

  1. They can fly at the altitude of 6000 feet
  2. Pigeons can fly at the speed of 50 to 60 miles per hour.
  3. Fastest known pigeon managed to reach speed on 92 miles per hour.

Teddy Bear

  • The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip
  • the Titanic sank in 1912, German toy company Steiff created 500 teddy bears to honor the victims. The “mourning bears” were black with red-rimmed eyes to show their sympathy. They now sell for $20,000 or more in auctions

Panda

  1. At 5 months old, giant pandas learn how to climb , sometimes practicing by climbing on their mum.
  2. They have an extended wrist bone that they use like a thumb to help them grip food.
  3. Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo.

Frog

  • There are over 4,700 species of frogs!
  • Many frogs can jump 20 times their own height.

Cicada

  • Cicadas shed their skin.
  • Cicadas are super loud. A bunch of male cicadas can get really loud!

Dogs

  1. dogs have been living with people for more than 9000 years
  2. dogs have been man’s most loyal friend
  3. A girl street dog called Laika. The Soviet Union sent her to space in the spaceship Sputnik 2. Poor Laika died only hours after the rocket’s take off but yet the experiment still could demonstrate that it is possible that living things would survive a trip to space
